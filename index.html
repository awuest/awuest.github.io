Welcome!

I am a first year masters student at Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health, where I study Health Data Science. Broadly, I am interested in applications of machine learning and model building in the sphere of health technology. During my time as an undergraduate at Florida State University, I worked on a community-oriented project to understand issues surrounding food, nutrition, and hunger in Leon County, FL. Coupled with over 4 years of research experience modeling populations dynamics and environmental phenomena, I have become specifically focused on how the environment and outside factors exacerbate health disparities in human populations. However, I hope to expand my expertise into the realms of management and consulting.

Highlights

Most of my major research projects as an undergraduate centered around environmental phenomena. I first learned to program my freshman year of college under Dr. Leithen M'Gonigle, a professor in the Biology department. We modeled fixation in two competing species in 2, 3, and 4 dimensions. I continued to work in the department under Dr. Andrew Rassweiler, where I modeled population dynamics of fish in French Polynesia. In 2018, I was awarded the Ernest F. Hollings scholarship through the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration. The following year I interned through NOAA at the Northwest Fisheries Science Center, under Dr. Jason Jannot, Dr. Tom Good, and Dr. Eric Ward. Here, I altered Dr. Ward's Bayesian bycatch model to predict the rates of Black-footed Albatross bycatch in Pacific longline fisheries. My course work at Harvard has been accented withed numerous public health projects. These include regression analysis for predicting women's likelihood to receive a pap-smear, analyzing and predicting mammography rates in the New York City metro area, and using the rjags package to predict inter-rate correlation coefficients.
